The Problem

The improper disposal of menstrual products poses a significant environmental problem due to the abundance of non-biodegradable plastic waste. Traditional disposable pads and tampons contain plastic components that take hundreds of years to decompose, contributing to landfill overflow and pollution. In regions with inadequate sanitation systems, the lack of proper waste management facilities exacerbates the issue, leading to clogged pipes, sewage problems, and environmental contamination. The production of these products also requires substantial resources like water, energy, and raw materials, contributing to deforestation, water pollution, and energy consumption.
